About Krishna Teachings

Lord Krishna, the eighth avatar of Lord Vishnu, was born in Mathura to Devaki and Vasudeva but was raised in Gokul by Yashoda and Nanda. His childhood was filled with divine miracles, such as lifting Govardhan Hill and slaying demons like Putana and Kansa. As he grew, Krishna became a central figure in the Mahabharata, guiding the Pandavas with wisdom and strength. His most profound teaching came through the **Bhagavad Gita**, where he imparted the principles of **Dharma, Karma, and Bhakti** to Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. He emphasized **detachment from material desires, selfless action, and devotion to God** as the path to liberation. As the ruler of Dwarka, he upheld justice and righteousness, playing a crucial role in shaping the course of history. Eventually, he left his mortal body, marking the end of the Dwapara Yuga. His teachings continue to inspire millions, guiding them toward a life of truth, devotion, and selfless service.
